Frequently Asked Questions

To verify your Google My Business listing:

1. Sign in to your Google My Business account
2. Select your business location
3. Click "Verify now" and choose your verification method
4. Complete the verification process (usually by postcard, phone, or email)
5. Wait for verification confirmation

Verification typically takes 1-7 business days depending on the method chosen.

Common reasons for GMB suspension include:

• Violating Google's quality guidelines
• Using a virtual office or PO Box for service area businesses
• Keyword stuffing in business name
• Fake reviews or review manipulation
• Duplicate listings
• Ineligible business type

To recover, identify the violation, fix the issue, and submit a reinstatement request through Google My Business support.

Ethical strategies to increase Google reviews:

• Ask satisfied customers directly after service
• Send follow-up emails with review links
• Display QR codes or review reminders in-store
• Provide excellent customer service consistently
• Make the review process easy with direct links
• Respond to all reviews professionally

Never buy fake reviews or incentivize only positive reviews, as this violates Google's policies.

Troubleshooting steps for poor visibility:

1. Verify your GMB listing is claimed and verified
2. Complete all available business information
3. Choose the most specific business categories
4. Add high-quality photos regularly
5. Encourage and respond to customer reviews
6. Post regular updates and offers
7. Ensure NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency across the web

Local SEO optimization takes time, typically 2-3 months to see significant improvements.

Category selection best practices:

• Choose the most specific primary category that describes your business
• Add relevant additional categories for services you offer
• Don't add categories that don't accurately represent your business
• Research what categories competitors use
• Consider how customers search for your services

Categories significantly impact your local search visibility, so choose carefully and update them as your business evolves.

Yes, you can manage multiple locations:

• Use Google My Business for multi-location management
• Each location needs its own unique listing
• Maintain consistent branding across all locations
• Customize content for local relevance
• Monitor each location's performance separately
• Consider using location-specific phone numbers

For 10+ locations, consider applying for Google My Business API access for bulk management capabilities.
